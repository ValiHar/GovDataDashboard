German Federal Ministries displayed by datasets provided to GovData.de

GovData.de is the data portal for Germany which intends to give the federal, state and local governments a platfrom to share their data. Although the "Open Data Act" makes it obligatory to share data, only about 52,000 data sets are online so far. This dashboard showcases the numbers of datasets that the individual federal ministries have shared via the public data portal so far.

Dataset distribution
ordered by the number of datasets shared by each ministry

The nightingale chart on the left illustrates the overall distribution of shared datasets over the German Federal Ministries.

To filter the chart, click on the ministry name in the legend. This will show/hide the clicked ministry in the diagram. You can also select/deselect all ministries or invert the diagram using the buttons on the left side of the legend.

Hovering the mouse cursor over the name of a ministry will display a tooltip indicating the exact number of records the ministry has shared and its percentage share in relation to all currently displayed ministries.

Distribution of datasets relative to neighbors

The following bar chart provides an additional way to examine the distribution of released datasets among German federal ministries. In the bar chart, ministries are sorted by the number of records they have released, as in the Nightingale chart.

However, the bar chart allows you to compare a ministry with its neighbors by zooming into the chart with the mouse wheel. When you move the mouse pointer over a ministry's bar, a tooltip appears with specific information about the ministry.
